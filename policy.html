Policy and Your Responsibility

Understanding clean indoor air rules for public spaces and how you can help protect everyone's health

Clean Indoor Air Rules

Indoor Public Spaces

No smoking or vaping in indoor public spaces including:

  • Classrooms and lecture halls
  • Offices and workspaces
  • Residence halls and dorms
  • Shared common areas
  • Libraries and study spaces
  • Dining facilities
  • Recreational facilities
  • Restrooms and lobbies

Building Entrances

Keep clear distance from building entrances and air intakes

  • Maintain 25 feet from main entrances
  • Stay away from air intake vents
  • Respect designated smoking areas only
  • Dispose of materials properly

Distance requirements protect everyone who uses building entrances

Why These Rules Protect Everyone

Immediate Protection

  • Prevents secondhand smoke exposure
  • Protects people with asthma and allergies
  • Keeps air clean for everyone
  • Reduces respiratory irritation

Long-term Health

  • Reduces heart disease risk
  • Prevents lung cancer in non-smokers
  • Protects children's development
  • Supports overall community health

Equal Access

  • Ensures everyone can use public spaces
  • Protects vulnerable populations
  • Creates inclusive environments
  • Supports disability accommodations

Your Responsibility

If You Smoke or Vape

  • Use only designated outdoor areas
  • Stay the required distance from entrances
  • Dispose of materials properly
  • Be considerate of people nearby
  • Consider quitting for your health and others

Everyone Can Help

  • Respect smoke-free spaces
  • Support friends who want to quit
  • Report violations to appropriate authorities
  • Be understanding and respectful
  • Share information about quit resources

Local Policy Information

Your Institution Smoking Policy

Indoor Spaces

All indoor spaces on campus are smoke-free and vape-free, including residence halls, academic buildings, dining facilities, and recreational areas.

Outdoor Restrictions

Smoking and vaping are prohibited within 25 feet of building entrances, air intakes, and windows.

Designated Areas

Smoking is permitted only in clearly marked designated areas. Check with your local administration for specific locations.

Enforcement

Violations may result in warnings, fines, or other disciplinary action as outlined in your local policy.

For complete policy details, contact your local administration or visit your institution's website.

Legal Framework

State and Local Laws

Most states have clean indoor air laws that prohibit smoking in public places. These laws exist to protect public health and ensure equal access to public spaces.

  • Enforceable by law enforcement
  • May include fines for violations
  • Apply to all public indoor spaces
  • Often include outdoor restrictions near entrances

Institutional Policies

Schools, workplaces, and other organizations often have their own tobacco-free policies that may be stricter than state law.

  • May prohibit all tobacco use on property
  • Can include disciplinary actions
  • Support health and safety goals
  • Create healthier environments for everyone
