Secondhand Smoke Harms

Clear health impacts for adults, children, and infants from secondhand smoke exposure

There is no safe level of exposure

Even brief exposure to secondhand smoke can cause immediate harm and deadly health problems.

Source: CDC

Adults

Immediate Effects

  • Increased risk of heart disease
  • Increased risk of stroke
  • Immediate effects on heart and blood vessels
  • Reduced oxygen in blood
  • Respiratory irritation
  • Eye and throat irritation

Long-term Risks

  • Heart disease
  • Stroke
  • Lung cancer
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • Increased risk of respiratory infections
  • Reduced lung function

Learn more from CDC →

Children

Respiratory Problems

  • Ear infections
  • Asthma attacks
  • Bronchitis
  • Pneumonia
  • More frequent and severe colds
  • Reduced lung function

Development Issues

  • Slower lung growth
  • Increased risk of developing asthma
  • Learning and behavioral problems
  • Higher risk of respiratory infections
  • Increased emergency room visits
  • More missed school days

Children are especially vulnerable because their lungs are still developing

Infants and Babies

Critical Risks

  • Sudden Infant Death Syndrome (SIDS)
  • Low birth weight
  • Premature birth
  • Birth defects
  • Stillbirth
  • Ectopic pregnancy

Health Problems

  • Respiratory infections
  • Bronchitis and pneumonia
  • Ear infections
  • Asthma
  • Reduced lung function
  • More hospitalizations

Babies face the highest risk for SIDS and serious respiratory problems

Protect Your Family

The only way to fully protect your family from secondhand smoke is to keep all indoor spaces completely smoke-free. If you smoke, quitting is the best thing you can do for your health and your family's health.
